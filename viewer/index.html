What am I looking at / Instructions / How to

Quick executive guide for using Scout Horizon without needing technical steps.

What am I looking at?

This page is a decision-support horizon console. It pulls signals from configured sources, scores them, and lets you narrow them down into a short list that matters for the leader right now.

  • Left side: what to search and how to filter it.
  • Center: the executive snapshot plus the clickable signal list.
  • Right side: the selected-signal explanation and recommended action.

How do I use it fast?

  1. Click Reset to demo defaults if you want a safe starting point.
  2. Click Run demo ingest to load fresh demo-safe signals.
  3. Use the Executive filters to narrow by region, lane, account, regulator, source, and score.
  4. Click any signal card to see why it matters and what to do next.

What do the numbers mean?

  • Score: overall relevance of the signal.
  • Confidence: how strong or corroborated the evidence appears.
  • Priority: how urgently it should rise for executive review.

Higher values rise toward the top when sorted by priority, score, or confidence.

How do I read the middle column?

Horizon Snapshot summarizes what is currently loaded and filtered. It shows how many signals are in view, average priority/confidence, and the top growth and risk themes.

Signal cards show the headline, source, date, preview text, and badges for score, confidence, and priority.

How do I read the right side?

When you click a signal, the right pane becomes your briefing sheet:

  • what happened
  • why it matters
  • recommended action
  • tags, labels, and corroboration
  • the source link to open externally

Best demo flow

  1. Reset to demo defaults
  2. Run demo ingest
  3. Set a region and lane
  4. Raise min priority to 0.70+
  5. Click the top 2–3 signals and read the recommended action aloud
Tip: If the screen ever feels too broad, use the quick chips first, then add one or two dropdown filters. That usually gets you to a clean executive shortlist very quickly.
